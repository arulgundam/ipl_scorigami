IPL Scorigami

This plot shows the first and second innings scores of all completed IPL games, including ones decided by the Duckworth–Lewis–Stern (DLS) method.

What is Scorigami?

Scorigami is a concept in sports, particularly popularized in American football, where a final score has never occurred before in the history of the sport. The concept was first created by American sportswriter Jon Bois.

To adapt NFL-style Scorigami to the IPL, or Indian Premier League, wickets fallen in each inning and balls faced in each inning have been ignored. The plot above solely shows the total scores in every IPL match in history.

Try clicking on each point in order to see the specific game that corresponds with the point on the plot! Our tool was made less to map distinct IPL scores, but instead to provide a unique directory to the competition's history.
